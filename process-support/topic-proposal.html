Sonar Brief Description

Sonar is a type of technique that uses sound to navigate and detect certain types of objects under water by emitting sound and measuring the objects return after being reflected. There are two types of Sonar, active and passive. Active sonar comprises a sound projector and a receiver. The projector also projects a sound and the receiver waits for a reflection. Nevertheless, passive sonar is a method of detecting acoustic signals in an underwater environment, such as the ocean

Relevance

Sonar is still relevant today since it can still be used to help scientists discover various types of hazards in the ocean and find new shipwrecks that were previously not discovered. It can still be used today to find sunken ships from world war 1 and world war 2.

Importance

Sonar tech has a number of uses in varying industries, such as for scientific application or for use in the fishing industry. The circumstances of a marine environment lends itself well to the sound-based sonar technology, which works better in denser mediums such as water.

Why we chose sonar?

We chose this topic and tech because as a group we had interest in learning about it as it is a fairly relevant theme in today's society. We believe it is a good idea to learn about a new topic to expand on our knowledge.
